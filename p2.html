Organizations Dedicated to Reducing GHG Emissions

Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC)

Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC)
About: The IPCC is an international body tasked with assessing the science related to climate change. It provides essential scientific information that can help governments implement climate policy.
Focus Areas: Climate assessment reports, policy-making guidance, global warming research.

Greenpeace

Greenpeace
About: Greenpeace is a non-governmental environmental organization with a global presence focused on direct action, lobbying, and research to achieve its goals.
Focus Areas: Climate change, deforestation, overfishing, commercial whaling, genetic engineering, and anti-nuclear issues.

The Sierra Club

The Sierra Club
About: Founded in 1892, the Sierra Club is one of the oldest and most influential grassroots environmental organizations in the United States.
Focus Areas: Promoting clean energy, making buildings and transportation more efficient, and mobilizing grassroots efforts to protect and restore natural landscapes.

The World Wildlife Fund (WWF)

The World Wildlife Fund (WWF)
About: WWF is an international non-governmental organization working in the field of wilderness preservation, and the reduction of human impact on the environment.
Focus Areas: Endangered species conservation, climate change mitigation, sustainable agriculture.

The Environmental Defense Fund (EDF)

The Environmental Defense Fund (EDF)
About: EDF works to solve the most critical environmental problems facing the planet. This includes climate change, oceans, ecosystems, and health.
Focus Areas: Climate, energy, ecosystems, oceans, health.
